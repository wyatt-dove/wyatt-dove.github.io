Classification: Buff

Description

You regenerate health and shields over time. Cannot be interrupted by taking damage.

Breakdown

While active, Restoration continuously restores health over time and can be extended to a maximum duration of 15 seconds by default. Restoration x1 / x2 restores 35 (17.5) / 50 (25) HP per second respectively.

When refreshing Restoration, it will prioritize the highest stack count and longest duration achieved (i.e. if 15 seconds of Restoration was achieved, using a Healing Grenade will refresh it to 15 seconds regardless of remaining time). This does not apply to duration extension effects, which extend the Restoration timer by a flat duration.

Classification: Debuff

Description

The target is singed by destructive Solar Light, taking damage over time. Scorch damage increases as the target accumulates more Scorch stacks.

After enough Scorch stacks are applied to the target, they ignite.

Breakdown

Targets with any amount of Scorch stacks will take a varying amount of damage every 0.56 seconds depending on the amount of Scorch stacks applied. Scorch can stack up to 100 times, and upon reaching this limit, all stacks are removed and a Solar Ignition is triggered.

Base Scorch damage per tick is calculated as follows:

  • 3 + (0.195 * stack count)
  • Non-boss combatants receive a 20% increase to Scorch damage
  • Scorch deals 2.5% increased damage after 60 Scorch stacks have been reached, indicated by yellow damage numbers
  • Enemy players receive a flat 3 / 5 / 6.5 / 7.21 / 8.64 Scorch damage at 1 / 30 / 50 / 60 / 80 stacks of Scorch, and Scorch damage cannot be fatal

Scorch decay over time is calculated as follows:

  • Scorch lasts for 4.5 seconds before falling off at a rate of 5.67 stacks every 0.56 seconds (approximately 10 stacks lost per second)
  • Against enemy players, Scorch decreases by 1 stack every 0.04 seconds (25 stacks per second)

The Ember of Ashes Solar Fragment increases the amount of Scorch applied from each source by a varying amount, indicated next to each source in parentheses below.

Classification: Debuff

Description

A large Solar explosion which deals damage in an area around the target and stuns Unstoppable Champions.

Breakdown

On creation, Ignitions deal 750 base Solar damage in a 10 meter radius with no damage falloff. Ignition damage will inherit damage bonuses from the source of the Ignition (i.e. an Incinerator Snap boosted by Winter’s Guile will boost the Ignition damage as well).

Repeated Ignitions within 5 seconds of each other against the same target will sharply reduce the damage dealt by each subsequent Ignition. Each Ignition deals 60% decreased damage, down to a maximum reduction of 97.3% after 4 Ignitions. This is a bug that will be fixed mid-November 2024.

Ignition damage will stun Unstoppable Champions.

Buffs

Classification: Buff

Description

Your movement speed and weapon handling are greatly increased. After sprinting for a short time, your movement speed is further increased. Rapidly defeating targets with Arc damage while any Arc ability is equipped makes you Amplified.

Breakdown

Amplified may be gained by incrementing a hidden counter in-game. This counter is incremented by getting Arc damage final blows within 6 seconds of each other. Tier 1 / 2 / 3+ enemies grant 25% / 50% / 100% counter progress per final blow. Alternatively, Amplified can be gained immediately through certain Aspects.

While Amplified, the player is granted 50 Mobility, 33% increased slide distance, 40 Handling and 0.95x handling duration multiplier for all equipped Weapons for 15 seconds. After sprinting for 2.5 seconds while Amplified, the Speed Booster buff is granted.

While Speed Booster is active, the player gains maximized movement speed, 50% increased slide distance, 25% increased jump height, and 15% damage resistance. Speed Booster will linger for 2 seconds after the player stops sprinting, and persists even if Amplified expires.

Classification: Buff

Description

You vanish from sight and do not appear on radar. While invisible, your radar range is reduced. Performing offensive actions will end invisibility.

Breakdown

While Invisibility is active, the player’s maximum radar range is reduced to 24 meters. Combatants are completely unable to target the player, instead targeting either their last known location or not attacking at all. Loud actions, such as double jumps, sprinting, or Dodging on a Hunter, can allow them to track the player, but not attack. Tracking projectiles are unable to track an Invisible player, and will lose their tracking ability if the player becomes Invisible while they are homing

Enemy players cannot see the Invisible player’s health bar, and the player will only ping radar every 5 seconds (or 0.6 seconds upon performing a loud action.

Actions that prematurely end Invisibility include firing weapons, using any ability aside from Hunter Dodges, and summoning a Vehicle.

Classification: Debuff

Description

The target is trapped in Stasis matter and is unable to move. After taking additional damage while frozen, the target shatters.

Breakdown

Frozen enemies are completely unable to shoot, use abilities, or move. While Frozen, all enemies receive 10% increased Special/Heavy Weapon damage, 5% increased Light Ability damage, and 5% (50%) reduced Primary Weapon damage. Tier 1 and 2 combatants as well as enemy players receive 120% increased Basic Melee Attack damage while Frozen.

Frozen enemies will Shatter after receiving varying damage in PvE and 200 damage in PvP.

Non-boss combatants will remain frozen for 6 seconds. Boss combatants are not immobilized while Frozen, and automatically Shatter after 3 seconds. Enemy players are Frozen for either 1.35 (Brief Freeze) or 4.75 seconds (Prolonged Freeze), depending on if the source of the Freeze is a non-Super or Super Ability respectively. While a player is Frozen, their Class Ability is replaced by a Breakout ability, which can prematurely free the player from being Frozen at the cost of some amount of Health.

Roaming Supers are briefly Frozen before automatically breaking out, while One-off Supers are deactivated completely.

Description

When Suspended, Unstoppable Champions become stunned.

Description

A knot of Strand matter, swirling with potential. Tangles can be shot and destroyed, or picked up and thrown, dealing damage. Defeating targets afflicted with any Strand debuff will unwind them and create a Tangle.

Breakdown

When any enemy is defeated while afflicted with any Strand debuff (Suspend, Sever, or Unravel), a Tangle is created on their position. Strand Tangles incur a 12 second cooldown upon creation and persist for up to 20 seconds or until picked up or destroyed.

Upon detonating a Tangle through damaging it or throwing it, an explosion is created that deals 385 (100) base damage in an approximate 5 meter radius.

Grappling to a Tangle fully refunds a Grenade charge, allowing players to Grapple to Tangles for free.

Some abilities create a unique Grapple Tangle, which allows free Grappling similarly to a regular Tangle, but cannot be picked up or destroyed. Grapple Tangles persist for up to 17.5 seconds, refreshing by 5 seconds upon being Grappled to by any player. This duration refresh is reduced to 1 second after 5 consecutive grapples.

Description

A creature woven from pure Strand matter that seeks out nearby targets and attacks, dealing damage.

A Threadling takes the appearance of a bug-like creature that travels independently along the ground, seeking enemies within approximately 20 meters. Upon being close enough to an enemy, the Threadling will leap at them and explode, dealing 338 (35) base damage in a small radius.

Warlocks gain the ability to possess Perched Threadlings. Threadlings count as damage from their source of creation (i.e. Weapon damage from Hatchling), but lose this inheritance upon Perching.

Prismatic allows you to combine all the strengths of each element into one, powerful subclass.

Utility

Classification: Utility Ability

Description

Achieve harmony by dealing damage with Light and Dark damage types in equal measure or by applying subclass buffs to your allies. Kinetic damage generates both Light and Dark energy. While Transcendence is active, your grenade is replaced by a unique Prismatic grenade that deals both Light and Dark damage. Dealing melee damage while transcendent greatly increases your grenade energy regeneration, and vice versa. Defeating targets while transcendent extends its duration.

Breakdown

While any Prismatic Subclass is equipped, the player gains two additional bars above their Super Ability which track Light and Dark energy. These bars charge independently of each other, and allow access to Transcendence when both are full.

Dealing Elemental damage and applying Elemental buffs and debuffs grants Transcendence energy for the corresponding bar. Kinetic Damage charges both Light and Darkness bars at 50% efficiency, and overkill damage does not count for charging Transcendence bars.

Upon filling both bars, Transcendence can be cast. On cast, Transcendence immediately refills up to 2 charges of Grenade and Melee Abilities. The Grenade Ability is replaced with a unique Prismatic Grenade with both Light and Dark damage capabilities.

  • Warlock: Freezing Singularity (Void/Suppress + Stasis/Slow)
  • Hunter: Hailfire Spike (Solar/Scorch + Stasis/Slow)
  • Titan: Electrified Snare (Arc/Jolt + Strand/Suspend)

While Transcendent, the player receives a 5% Weapon damage boost, 10% Grenade and Melee Regeneration speed, and 20% damage resistance for 20 seconds. Dealing Grenade Ability damage increases Melee Ability Regeneration speed to a flat 35% ability energy per second for 2 seconds, refreshing with additional damage. This same benefit applies the other way around with Melee Ability damage for Grenade Ability Regeneration speed.

Obtaining final blows while Transcendent extends its duration, with diminishing returns on subsequent final blows until Transcendence expires. Both Light and Dark bars are completely reset once Transcendence expires.
